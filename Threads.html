Threads and Sockets Study Guide

  • Understand the challenges of parallelization w/ regard to shared resources: Parallelization introduces challenges such as race conditions, deadlocks, and resource contention when multiple threads access shared resources concurrently.
  • Understand the mechanics of multithreading: Multithreading involves executing multiple threads concurrently within a single process. Threads share code, data, and files but have their own call stack and registers.
  • Understand the producer/consumer problem outlined in Unit 7: The producer/consumer problem is a classic synchronization problem where one or more threads produce data items and put them into a shared buffer, while other threads consume these items from the buffer. It requires synchronization mechanisms like locks or semaphores to ensure proper coordination between producers and consumers.
  • Understand the Unit 9 example code: Unit 9 likely contains example code related to threads and sockets. Understanding this code helps in grasping concepts such as socket programming, thread creation, synchronization, and communication between threads over a network.
