Introduction

The tables below serve as a reference to basic regex. If you're in doubt while reading the rest of the site, you can always come back and refer to this page. The tables aren't exhaustive to avoid overcrowding with exotic syntax. It's recommended to refer to the specific regex flavor documentation for a full reference. These tables are designed to provide a quick introduction to regex, progressing logically from basic to more advanced features.

For those suffering from online-ADD, it's advisable to print the tables for better focus and easy reference later.

How to Use the Tables

The tables are meant to be read slowly, one line at a time. Each line introduces a new element of regex syntax, followed by a legend explaining its meaning, an example demonstrating its usage, and a sample match to illustrate the result.
